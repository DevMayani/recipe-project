The Origin of Spaghetti Bolognese

So What's The History Behind Spaghetti Bolognese?

It should be noted that the origin of pasta Bolognese does not involve spaghetti at all.
Instead, the name comes from an initial recipe in Bologna, involving Tagliatelle and a rich rag첫.

In Italy, rag첫 is a term used to describe a type of meat sauce that has been cooked for many hours over low heat. Each rag첫 boasts numerous ingredients, which varies from region to region. In fact, "alla bolognese" is only one of the many different rag첫s prepared in Italy.

INGREDIENTS Measurement
Olive oil 1 tbsp
Chopped bacon Choice
Chopped onions 2
Chopped carrots 2
Chopped celery sticks 2
Chopped garlic cloves 2
Dried oregano 1 tsp
Bay leaves 2
Tomato paste 2 tbsp
Chilli pepper, chopped 1 red
Red wine 125ml
Tomatoes, sliced in half 6
Spaghetti Choice

METHOD

  1. Add spaghetti into a pot almost filled with water. Add salt to the spaghetti.
  2. Allow boil for fifteen minutes or until the spaghetti softens. Drain the water from the cooked spaghetti and set aside.
  3. Heat the oil in a pot and add bacon and fry until it turns golden.
  4. Reduce the heat and then add the carrots, celery sticks, onions and garlic cloves. Fry the vegetables for 10 minutes.
  5. Add the minced beef and cook until the meat turns brown.
  6. Add the tomato paste, the bay leaves, tomatoes, the chopped red chilli pepper and the red wine.
  7. Stir with a spoon. Cook for 1 hour 15 minutes stirring occasionally, until you have a rich, thick sauce. The Bolognese sauce is ready! Serve with spaghetti.
